## Business Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Task | A unit of work that can be created, assigned, tracked, and completed. |
| Owner | The primary responsible party for a task. |
| Assignee | The person currently responsible for executing a task. |
| Team | A collaborative group of users working on shared work. |
| Archive | A controlled state that makes a task read-only except for authorized restoration. |
| Notification | A message that informs a user about an activity or reminder. |
| Dashboard | A summary workspace showing workload, deadlines, overdue work, and team activity. |
| Activity History | An immutable record of task changes and collaboration actions. |
| Role | A business-defined access level that governs what a user can do. |
| Reminder | A business alert that signals due-date risk or pending action. |

## Domain Definitions
- Collaboration: Coordinated work across users and teams to achieve shared outcomes.
- Traceability: The ability to connect business requirements to delivered behavior and validation.
- Governance: The business and operational controls that protect data integrity and accountability.
- Workspace: The user-facing area where tasks, dashboards, and team activity are managed.

## Key Concepts
- Accountability: Each task should have a clear owner and visible history.
- Visibility: Users should be able to understand status, risk, and workload at a glance.
- Security: Access and actions must align with users' roles and permissions.
